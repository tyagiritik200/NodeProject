ABOUT



U Mumba is the Mumbai city franchise of the Pro Kabaddi League. Derived from the word Mumba, the name represents the mother island that gave birth to our mega city which is now known as Mumbai. We the citizens make the city what it is, therefore the name U Mumba. The team chose orange as it's primary colour for their jersey because fisher folks of the region use this as their primary identifiable colour, this personifies the fact that in the deep blue ocean orange stands out as a clutter breaker... that is U Mumba for the rest of the world.


Background


U Mumba is owned by Unilazer Sports, which is a company run by Ronnie Screwvala, the founder of UTV Group. U Sports spans its reaches not only to Kabaddi but to Football, ESports and Motorsports. Their future plans include constructing an U Mumba arena, reaching out to top metropolitan cities in India and building a U Mumba residential Academy in the name of U Mumba Gurukul.


Brief History: Season 1 to 6


The team was led by Captain Anup Kumar for all 5 seasons, coached by Bhaskaran Edacherry and Ravi Shetty.
In season 1, the team finished Runners-Up, losing out to eventual champions Jaipur Pink Panthers in a closely fought finale.
In season 2, U Mumba consistently topped the table during the league stage and beat Bengaluru Bulls in a thrilling finale to be crowned Star Sports Pro Kabaddi League 2015 Champions.
In season 3, U Mumba topped the table again during the league stage to qualify for the semi-finals. In the semi-finals, U Mumba beat Bengal Warriors to qualify for their third successive final. Up against the Patna Pirates, U Mumba lost.
In season 4, U Mumba stood 5th on the table, unable to make it to the Playoffs.
In season 5, U Mumba finished 8th on the team performance table.
In 2018, season 6 the team has been reimagined and recreated from a scratch. We now have a new set of coaches namely Gholamreza Mahmoud Mazandarani and Upendra Kumar. The idea was to focus on a young dynamic squad with a good mix of youth and experience. The team will comprise of an experienced defensive (corners and covers) line up with a young offence (raiders) unit.
